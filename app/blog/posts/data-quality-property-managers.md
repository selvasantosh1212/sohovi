# Data Quality for Property Managers: Fix Your Tenant Database This Weekend

The eviction notice went to the wrong address. The maintenance request was filed under the wrong unit. The rent statement shows the tenant still owes a balance from a payment that was made six weeks ago but recorded against the wrong account.

These aren't rare horror stories — they're the daily cost of a tenant database that hasn't been maintained. For a property manager handling 50–500 units, data quality problems create friction in every tenant interaction, every maintenance workflow, and every financial report.

Here's how to assess and fix the most impactful problems in a weekend.

## The Most Common Property Manager Data Quality Problems

### Tenant Contact Information That's Out of Date

A tenant who moved in two years ago may have changed their phone number, switched to a different email address, or updated their emergency contact. Notices sent to stale contact information are legally risky — if a required notice doesn't reach the tenant because your records are wrong, the legal consequences can be significant.

### Unit Assignments That Don't Match Reality

A unit showing as occupied when it's been vacant for three weeks. A unit showing as vacant when a month-to-month tenant is still there. A tenant assigned to the wrong unit number due to a data entry error.

These errors produce wrong occupancy reports and wrong financial projections.

### Lease Dates and Terms With Errors

A lease end date entered incorrectly creates a situation where a tenant receives a renewal notice too early, too late, or not at all. A wrong lease start date affects pro-rata rent calculations. A wrong rent amount in the system produces wrong billing.

### Duplicate Tenant Records

A tenant who had a previous tenancy (perhaps they renewed after a gap, or returned after moving away) may have two records — one from the previous tenancy, one from the current. Split records mean split payment history, incomplete maintenance history, and potential for duplicate billing.

### Maintenance Request Orphan Records

Maintenance requests submitted through a portal or ticketing system that aren't linked to the correct unit or tenant create follow-up gaps. An open maintenance request with no tenant assignment is easy to overlook.

### Payment Records With Wrong Allocations

A tenant's payment applied to the wrong unit (especially in large portfolios), or a deposit allocated to rent instead of the deposit holding account. Wrong allocations produce wrong balances and billing disputes.

## The Weekend Tenant Database Fix

### Saturday Morning: Export and Profile (2–3 Hours)

Export your tenant database including: unit address/number, tenant name, email, phone, lease start date, lease end date, monthly rent amount, current balance, and active status.

Check for:

**Completeness**: Which tenants have no email? No phone? Missing lease dates?

**Duplicates**: Are any tenant names (or email addresses) appearing more than once?

**Date logic**: Are there any leases where the end date is before the start date? (Data entry error) Are there leases with end dates that have already passed but are still marked as active?

**Rent amount plausibility**: Are there any units with $0 rent (accounted for error)? Any with obviously wrong amounts (far below or above your typical range for that unit type)?

Document all findings.

### Saturday Afternoon: Fix the Critical Issues (3–4 Hours)

Work through your findings in priority order:

**Critical (fix today):**
- Tenants with no contact information and open lease
- Units showing wrong occupancy status
- Lease dates that are clearly wrong

**High priority:**
- Duplicate tenant records — merge with history consolidated
- Tenants with only one contact method — flag to collect the other at next interaction

**Standard maintenance:**
- Format inconsistencies in phone numbers and addresses
- Missing secondary contact information

### Sunday: Verify and Update Contact Information (2 Hours)

For tenants with missing or suspect contact information:
- A quick check of your communications history — is there an email address in a recent communication that isn't in the main record?
- For cases where records have been stale for a year or more, schedule a brief tenant check-in

Document what was verified and when. Add a "contact verified" date field if your system supports it.

## Building Maintenance Habits

The weekend fix addresses accumulated problems. Preventing recurrence requires process changes:

**Lease renewal process**: Before any lease is renewed, verify and update tenant contact information as a step in the renewal workflow.

**Move-in checklist**: Include required data fields in your move-in documentation. Collect email, mobile phone, and emergency contact at lease signing — not as an afterthought.

**Monthly payment reconciliation**: At each month-end, confirm all payments are allocated to the correct unit and tenant. Flag any discrepancies before they accumulate.

**Vacancy update protocol**: The moment a unit is vacated, update the unit status. Assign responsibility for this step so it doesn't fall through the cracks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do I handle GDPR or state privacy laws for tenant data?**
Tenant data is personal data subject to applicable privacy laws. Handle exports and quality reviews in secure environments. Don't share tenant data with third parties without a data processing basis. Respond to tenant requests to correct inaccurate records (most privacy laws give individuals this right).

**Q: Should I delete records for past tenants?**
No — retain them for the legally required period (typically 3–7 years after tenancy ends, depending on your jurisdiction and the records type). Archive them in your system with a "Former Tenant" status rather than deleting.

**Q: What property management software has the best data quality features?**
Buildium, AppFolio, Yardi Breeze, and Rent Manager all have robust data management. For small landlords (under 20 units), a well-maintained spreadsheet or simple tool like Landlord Studio can be sufficient if maintained consistently.
