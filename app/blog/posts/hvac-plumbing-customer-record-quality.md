# How HVAC and Plumbing Companies Can Improve Customer Record Quality

Your HVAC company serviced Mrs. Johnson's unit three years ago. Today she calls back because the system needs a new part. Your technician has no record of the previous service, doesn't know what model was installed, and spends 20 minutes gathering information she already gave you three years ago.

She hired the other HVAC company last year. Your records never told you her annual service contract was up for renewal.

For home service companies — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pest control — customer record quality isn't just an operational issue. It's the foundation of repeat business, service contract renewal, and the customer experience that drives referrals.

## The Data Quality Problems Specific to Field Service Businesses

### Incomplete Service History

A customer with four service calls over five years should have a complete history: what was serviced, what equipment, what was found, what was done, what was installed. When service records are incomplete — missing model numbers, incomplete diagnosis notes, unlinked follow-up visits — the next technician starts from scratch every time.

This makes customers repeat themselves, makes technicians less efficient, and signals organizational disorganization.

### Equipment Records Without Details

For HVAC companies, knowing the equipment installed is the foundation of service. A customer record that shows "central air unit" without brand, model, serial number, install date, or last service date is nearly useless for proactive outreach.

Without equipment records, you can't:
- Reach out when a model commonly has service issues
- Know when a system is approaching end-of-life for replacement conversations
- Accurately quote service without a site visit to identify the equipment

### Wrong or Outdated Service Addresses

A customer who moved, or a rental property that changed tenants, may have the wrong service address on file. Scheduling a technician to a wrong address wastes their time and creates a poor first impression (arriving at an address and finding a confused homeowner is a common field service problem).

### Duplicate Customer Records

A customer who called in two years ago had a record created by the office. They called again with a different number and a different person created another record. Two records, split history.

### Unintelligible Job Notes

Job notes written by one technician that only that technician can interpret. "Changed part — fixed" tells the next technician nothing useful. Inconsistent note formats make service history non-searchable and unreliable.

## The Customer Database Improvement Plan

### Phase 1: Standardize What You Capture

Define the required fields for every customer record and every job record:

**Customer record required fields:**
- Full legal name (for billing and contracts)
- Service address (full, verified)
- Primary phone (mobile preferred for text reminders)
- Email address
- Billing address if different from service address
- Customer since date

**Job record required fields:**
- Job date
- Technician assigned
- Equipment: brand, model, serial number (for HVAC/appliance work)
- Problem reported
- Work performed (specific, searchable)
- Parts replaced (with part numbers if available)
- Follow-up required? Y/N
- Customer signature/approval

### Phase 2: Clean Existing Records

Export your customer database and run a quality check:

**Completeness**: Which records are missing email addresses? Missing phone? Missing service address?

**Duplicates**: Check for customers with the same name and address appearing more than once.

**Service address accuracy**: For customers with service dates more than 2 years ago and no recent activity, flag for confirmation before sending any outreach.

**Equipment record completeness**: For HVAC/appliance customers, what percentage of records have equipment brand/model recorded? Low rates here are a service efficiency problem.

For large databases (1,000+ customers), Sohovi can profile your exported CSV and show completeness and duplicate rates per column in under a minute — useful baseline before manual cleanup.

### Phase 3: Build the Capture Habit

**At job booking**: Collect email address and verify service address. "What's the best email address for appointment confirmations and reminders?"

**At job completion**: Technician completes the job record form before leaving the site — not back at the shop, not at end of day. Mobile apps for field service software (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber) support this workflow.

**Post-job follow-up**: An automated follow-up text or email serves double duty: it gets the customer feedback AND confirms that the contact information is working.

## Proactive Use of Good Customer Records

Clean customer data enables proactive service outreach:

**Annual service reminders**: For HVAC customers, a "time for your annual tune-up" message requires knowing their equipment type and last service date.

**End-of-warranty outreach**: For customers with equipment at or near warranty expiration, a proactive service agreement offer at the right time converts well.

**Weather-triggered outreach**: When a heat wave or cold snap is forecast, companies with accurate equipment records can prioritize outreach to customers with older or recently flagged equipment.

All of this requires clean, complete customer and equipment records. Without them, it's all manual guesswork.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What software do HVAC and plumbing companies use for customer records?**
ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, and FieldEdge are purpose-built for field service businesses. They handle customer records, job management, invoicing, and field communication in one system. For small operations (under 10 technicians), simpler tools like Jobber work well.

**Q: Should we enter records for one-time customers?**
Yes — with less detail than for repeat customers, but with complete contact information. One-time customers become repeat customers when you follow up at the right time.

**Q: How do we handle customers who call from different phones and we create duplicate records?**
Search before creating. Train intake staff to search by both name and address before creating a new record. When duplicates are found, merge them with service history consolidated.
