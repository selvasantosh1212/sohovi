# How Mortgage Brokers Can Validate Client Data Before Submission

A loan application with wrong income figures, mismatched addresses, or an inconsistent SSN can produce conditions that delay closing by weeks, or denials that damage the client relationship and your reputation.

Most data errors in loan applications aren't fraud — they're honest mistakes in data entry, transcription from documents, or inconsistencies between sources. The broker who catches them before submission delivers a smoother process. The broker who doesn't spends the week after submission chasing conditions and explaining delays to an anxious client.

## Common Data Quality Errors in Mortgage Applications

### Name and SSN Mismatches

The application has the borrower's name spelled one way; the credit report has it slightly differently; the title commitment has another variation. When names don't exactly match across documents, underwriters request explanations — a condition that delays closing.

SSN transpositions are less common but high-stakes: a wrong SSN produces a credit report for the wrong person, which creates both a processing delay and a potential privacy issue.

### Address Inconsistencies

The subject property address entered on the application differs from the way it appears on the purchase agreement or title report. "123 Main Street" vs. "123 Main St." may be the same address, but the inconsistency triggers an underwriting question.

Employment addresses that don't match what the employer verifies. Residency addresses that don't match the lease or prior tax return.

### Income Calculation Errors

Self-employed borrower income calculated differently on the application vs. what the tax returns support. Bonus income included at the wrong frequency. Two-year average income computed on one-year documents.

Income errors are the most common cause of loan conditions and the most likely to produce denials. Calculation errors that favor the borrower become compliance issues; calculation errors that harm the borrower create disputes.

### Asset Account Data Errors

Wrong account numbers on the asset documentation. Account balance that doesn't match the most recent statement because the statement wasn't the most current. IRA/retirement accounts included at full value when the policy is to apply a haircut for early withdrawal penalties.

### Employment Verification Mismatches

A job title that doesn't match what the employer verifies. A start date that's off by one month. A company name that's an informal version of the legal name.

### Compliance Data Completeness

HMDA (Home Mortgage Disclosure Act) fields — race, ethnicity, sex — that are blank when they should be "borrower declined to provide" or filled in correctly. These aren't loan approval factors, but regulators check HMDA data completeness.

## The Pre-Submission Validation Checklist

Before submitting any loan application, run through these validation steps:

### Identity and Credit Data

- [ ] Borrower name on application exactly matches ID documents
- [ ] SSN on application verified against source document (not transcribed from memory)
- [ ] Co-borrower information (if applicable) verified separately
- [ ] Credit score and report matches the borrower profile submitted

### Property Data

- [ ] Subject property address exactly matches the purchase agreement and title order
- [ ] Property type, use, and legal description are consistent across all documents
- [ ] Appraised value (if available) consistent with application

### Income Documentation

- [ ] Income figures match the supporting documents page by page
- [ ] Self-employment income calculation documented and follows guidelines
- [ ] All income types included are eligible per loan guidelines
- [ ] Two-year average correctly calculated where required

### Asset Documentation

- [ ] Asset accounts match the statements provided
- [ ] Account balances are from statements within the required timeframe
- [ ] Retirement/investment accounts applied at the correct haircut (if applicable)
- [ ] Gift funds documented with gift letter if applicable

### Employment Data

- [ ] Employer name matches official verification (not an informal variation)
- [ ] Employment start date matches documentation
- [ ] Title and status (full-time, part-time, self-employed) correctly coded

## How to Minimize Data Entry Errors at Intake

**Use auto-population where possible**: Most modern loan origination systems (LOS) can pull borrower data from documents electronically. Use these features to reduce manual transcription.

**Verify at data entry, not at submission**: Each time a document is received and entered, verify the entered data against the document before moving on. Catching a transposition at entry takes 30 seconds; catching it at submission means reprocessing.

**Keep a single source of truth**: For borrower information, designate one location (the LOS) as authoritative. Don't maintain parallel records in a spreadsheet and the LOS — they will diverge.

**Pre-submission audit checklist**: Use a physical or digital checklist (like the one above) before every submission. Checklists work precisely because they don't rely on memory.

**Client-facing data review**: Before submission, have the borrower review the key application data: name, address, income, assets. A 10-minute call where the borrower confirms their own information catches discrepancies the broker couldn't have known about.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who is responsible when a data error causes a condition or delay?**
Practically: the broker manages the remedy. Professionally: whoever entered or failed to verify the data. A systematic pre-submission checklist creates a documented standard of care that protects the broker in disputes.

**Q: How should I handle it when the client's documents have inconsistencies?**
Document them and resolve them before submission with an explanation letter if needed. "Borrower's name appears as 'Robert' on tax returns and 'Bob' on the purchase agreement — these refer to the same person (Robert 'Bob' Johnson, SSN XXX-XX-XXXX)" preemptively addresses what would otherwise become a condition.

**Q: What's the most common data error that causes loan conditions?**
Address inconsistencies and name variations are the most frequent minor conditions. Income calculation differences between the application and the documentation are the most common material conditions — the ones that cause the most delay.

**Q: Should I maintain my own data quality records separate from the LOS?**
Your LOS is the authoritative record for all loan data. Don't maintain parallel records. If the LOS has data quality limitations, work with your LOS provider or workflow to address them — don't create a shadow system that will diverge.
