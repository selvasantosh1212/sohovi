# How to Prepare Your Customer Data Before Black Friday / Cyber Monday

Black Friday and Cyber Monday are when your customer list has to work hardest. You're sending more emails than any other time of year, running personalized campaigns based on purchase history, and spending more on paid audiences than any other quarter.

Bad data at this moment is more expensive than at any other moment. A 15% email bounce rate during BFCM can trigger deliverability warnings that affect your ability to send for weeks afterward. Wrong purchase history produces mis-targeted campaigns. Duplicate customer records send the same person three emails in a week — increasing unsubscribes at exactly the wrong time.

Here's the customer data preparation checklist to run six to eight weeks before BFCM.

## Why Pre-BFCM Matters More Than Other Times

**Higher send volume amplifies problems**: A 3% bounce rate matters more when you're sending 10 email campaigns in a week than when you're sending one per month.

**Higher ad spend amplifies targeting errors**: Paid campaigns against a customer list with 15% duplicates are wasting 15% of your ad budget on the same people twice.

**Customer attention is at its peak**: First impressions during BFCM matter. Customers who receive a double email, a campaign about a product they already bought, or mail to an address they moved from two years ago form opinions about your brand that don't reset quickly.

**Competition is at its highest**: If a deliverability problem reduces your email reach, you lose to competitors during the most important revenue period. There's no "we'll catch up next week."

## The Six-Week Pre-BFCM Data Checklist

### 6 Weeks Out: Audit the Full Customer List

Export your full customer database and run a quality profile. You're looking for:

**Completeness of email addresses**: What percentage of customers have a valid email on file? Anyone without an email can't receive email campaigns.

**Email format validity**: How many email addresses fail basic format checks? (No "@", obvious typos, invalid domains) These should be cleaned or removed before the BFCM send.

**Duplicate customer accounts**: How many customers appear more than once? (Same email on two accounts, or same name/phone with different emails) Deduplicate before building your BFCM audiences.

**Completeness of shipping addresses**: For customers you'll send direct mail to, or for customers where accurate address data affects personalization (regional promotions, local delivery, etc.) — how many have complete addresses?

**Recency distribution**: How many customers last purchased more than 24 months ago? You may want to segment them differently (re-engagement campaign vs. BFCM main sequence).

### 4 Weeks Out: Clean What You Found

Based on your audit:

**Remove or fix invalid emails**: Invalid email addresses will hard-bounce. Remove them from your BFCM audiences or fix them if the error is correctable (obvious typo like "gmial.com" → "gmail.com").

**Deduplicate**: Merge or remove duplicate customer records. For BFCM, you at minimum need to deduplicate your email send list — one email per person.

**Segment by recency**: Separate customers into segments based on last purchase date. Recent customers (12 months) get the main BFCM sequence. Older customers (12–24 months) get a win-back offer. Very old customers (24+ months) might be excluded from email campaigns to protect deliverability, or sent a single re-engagement email first.

### 3 Weeks Out: Validate Your Email List

For lists that haven't been emailed in 3+ months, or for any customer segments that haven't been active recently, run the list through an email verification service (ZeroBounce, NeverBounce, or similar).

These services check whether email addresses are still deliverable at the server level — a critical check for list hygiene before high-volume sending.

Remove all addresses classified as "invalid" or "do not send." This reduces your list size but improves deliverability for the addresses that remain.

### 2 Weeks Out: Build and Test Your Segments

With a clean list:
- Build your BFCM audience segments (active customers, lapsed customers, VIPs, by category purchase history)
- Verify the segment sizes look correct (if your "customers who bought category X" segment is much larger or smaller than expected, investigate)
- Send a small test send to each segment to verify deliverability and rendering

### 1 Week Out: Final Checks

- Verify your email sending domain and sender reputation (check your domain's spam score — tools like Google Postmaster Tools or MXToolbox)
- Confirm your email platform hasn't flagged any deliverability warnings
- Do a final list size check — your audience for each campaign should be what you expect

## The Direct Mail Pre-BFCM Checklist

If you're sending physical mail (catalogs, postcards, discount cards) for BFCM:

- Validate all mailing addresses against USPS NCOA (National Change of Address) database — standard through most mailing services
- Remove invalid addresses (no street number, invalid ZIP, incomplete apartment information)
- Check for duplicates — the same customer at the same address shouldn't receive two pieces

Physical mail costs $0.50–$2.00+ per piece. Sending to bad addresses is wasted spend.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How early should I start cleaning data before BFCM?**
Six weeks is the minimum. Email deliverability issues can take 2–4 weeks to resolve if you've already triggered warnings from high bounce rates. Starting early gives you time to clean the list before any warming period or deliverability issue affects your peak sends.

**Q: What's a safe email bounce rate during BFCM?**
Keep hard bounce rates below 2%. Above that, most email platforms will warn or restrict your sending. Above 5%, you risk suspension. If your pre-BFCM audit finds bounce rates above 1%, clean the list before sending.

**Q: Is it worth running email verification for a smaller list (under 2,000 contacts)?**
Yes, especially if the list hasn't been mailed recently. The cost of verification for 2,000 records is roughly $2–$20. The cost of triggering a deliverability warning during BFCM is much higher.

**Q: Should I try to re-engage inactive customers for BFCM?**
A targeted re-engagement campaign to lapsed customers (sent 4–6 weeks before BFCM) can recapture some of them. But don't send BFCM emails to customers who didn't respond to the re-engagement campaign — they're likely to bounce or mark as spam.
