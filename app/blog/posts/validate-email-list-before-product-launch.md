# How to Validate Your Email List Before a Product Launch

A product launch email campaign has one chance to be timely. Unlike a regular newsletter where a quality problem just hurts one send, a launch campaign that underdelivers because of list quality issues misses a moment that doesn't come back.

The subscriber who was genuinely interested in your launch but whose email address bounced might have bought on that day. That revenue is gone.

Here's how to validate your email list in the two weeks before a major product launch.

## Why Launch Campaigns Need Extra Scrutiny

Regular email programs benefit from consistent list hygiene. But product launches have specific characteristics that make pre-launch validation especially important:

**High opportunity cost**: Launch day is when interest and intent are highest. List quality problems that reduce delivery directly reduce launch revenue.

**Often sending to a broader or less-active segment**: Launch campaigns sometimes go to subscribers who haven't been emailed recently — an announcement list, a waitlist, or a broader base than your regular sends. These segments are more likely to have stale addresses.

**Public commitment to reach**: Launch campaigns are often tied to business goals and investor or stakeholder expectations. Underperformance needs explanation, and list quality problems are preventable.

**Higher send volume than usual**: Many senders email more frequently during a launch window. More sends means more opportunities for quality problems to compound.

## The Two-Week Pre-Launch Validation Checklist

### Two Weeks Out: Baseline Assessment

**Export and profile the full send list**. For each segment you're planning to use:
- Completeness of email addresses
- Format validity check
- Duplicate email addresses
- Age distribution (when was each contact added?)

Identify any segments with high proportions of old addresses (added 18+ months ago) — these are your highest-risk segments.

**Check your most recent campaign metrics**. What were the bounce rates on your last few sends? If recent campaigns had elevated bounce rates, the problem is already present and needs to be addressed before launch.

**Identify the high-stakes segment**: Which segment is expected to produce the most revenue? That's the segment you invest the most validation effort in.

### Ten Days Out: Verification

**Run your high-stakes segments through an email verification service**. For any segment that:
- Contains contacts added more than 12 months ago
- Hasn't been emailed in the past 3 months
- Includes contacts from external sources (list imports, partner data, purchased lists)

A verification service (ZeroBounce, NeverBounce, Kickbox) classifies each address as deliverable, risky, or invalid. Remove the invalid and risky addresses before launch.

**Verify your technical setup**:
- Is SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured correctly for your sending domain?
- Is your sending domain on any blocklists? (Check with MXToolbox)
- Is your IP reputation healthy? (Check Google Postmaster Tools if you're sending significant volume to Gmail)

Technical problems caught 10 days before launch are fixable. Caught on launch day, they're disasters.

### One Week Out: Segment Confirmation

**Confirm the exact audience for each launch email**:
- What are the segment definitions?
- How many contacts are in each segment after validation?
- Are the audiences mutually exclusive where they should be? (Ensure that contacts in one segment aren't also receiving a different version)

**Run a final duplicate check**: One email address should appear in the launch send only once. If the same person is in multiple segments, define the de-duplication rule (which email do they receive?) before the send.

**Prepare your suppression list**: All previous unsubscribers, hard-bounce addresses, and any explicitly excluded contacts should be in your suppression list and confirmed applied to the launch send.

### Three Days Out: Send a Warmup or Preview

**If possible, send a warmup email** to a subset of your list before launch day. An announcement of the upcoming launch, a teaser, or a "we're getting ready" message:
- Re-engages the list before the launch ask
- Surfaces any remaining deliverability issues before the most important send
- Generates pre-launch interest that can improve launch day conversions

**Check warmup metrics carefully**: Any bounce rate above 1% on the warmup send indicates remaining list quality problems. Address them before the full launch send.

## Day-Of Launch Checklist

Before the launch email goes:
- [ ] Final segment sizes are as expected (significant deviations need investigation)
- [ ] Suppression list is confirmed applied
- [ ] Test send received correctly (including all personalization and dynamic content)
- [ ] From name and subject line match the approved campaign brief
- [ ] Unsubscribe link and physical address are present and working

## Monitoring During and After Launch

Monitor the first 2 hours of the launch send:
- Is the delivery rate above 95%?
- Is the bounce rate below 1%?
- Are there any platform warnings or delivery rate flags?

If bounce rate spikes during the send, your email platform may allow you to pause, investigate, and resume — catching the problem before it compounds.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I don't have two weeks before launch?**
With limited time, prioritize: (1) remove known hard-bounce addresses from previous sends, (2) verify your technical setup (SPF, DKIM, DMARC, blocklist check), (3) run a quick format validation pass. Do what you can with the time you have — even partial validation is better than none.

**Q: Should I send the launch email to my full list or just active subscribers?**
This is a deliverability vs. reach tradeoff. Sending to active subscribers only (opened or clicked in the last 12 months) protects deliverability but reduces reach. Sending to your full validated list maximizes reach but carries slightly higher deliverability risk. For launches, a validated full list typically produces better net results — just make sure the validation is thorough.

**Q: How do I handle a waitlist that was collected specifically for this launch?**
Treat a waitlist like any other email list that needs validation before sending. People on a waitlist gave their email for a purpose, so consent is clear — but their addresses may have changed, and the list may have been collected using a form without email verification. Run a format check and consider verification if the list is large.
